What is a SACCO?

Savings and Credit Cooperative Organizations (SACCOs) are member-owned financial institutions where people save together and access affordable loans.

Examples in Kenya:

  • Safaricom Kona SACCO
  • Stima SACCO
  • Mwalimu SACCO

Benefits: Low-interest loans, collective saving power, annual dividends.

Money Market Funds (MMFs)

MMFs pool members’ funds into short-term, safe investments like Treasury bills. They are low-risk and provide steady returns.

Popular MMFs in Kenya:

  • Britam Money Market Fund
  • KCB M-Pesa (MMF option)
  • Zidii Money Market Fund

Benefits: Liquidity (easy withdrawals), low risk, steady returns (8–11% annually).

Stocks & Investments

Buying shares in a company makes you a part-owner. Stocks can grow in value and may also pay dividends. They carry higher risk but can bring higher returns.

Commonly Traded in Kenya:

  • Safaricom PLC
  • Equity Bank
  • KCB Group

Benefits: Potential for high returns, dividend payments.

Pro-Tips from EduFin Mentor

  • Start small — consistency matters more than size.
  • Diversify — combine SACCO, MMF, and stocks for balance.
  • Set financial goals and stick to them.
  • Always research before investing.
