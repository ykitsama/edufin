What is a SACCO?

Savings and Credit Cooperative Organizations (SACCOs) are member-owned financial institutions where people save together and access affordable loans. They encourage a culture of saving while providing members with affordable credit options.

Examples in Kenya:

  • Safaricom Kona SACCO
  • Stima SACCO
  • Mwalimu SACCO

Benefits: Low-interest loans, collective saving power, annual dividends.

Money Market Funds (MMFs)

MMFs are collective investment schemes where members pool funds that are invested in safe, short-term assets such as Treasury bills, commercial papers, and government securities. They are low-risk and provide steady interest returns.

Popular MMFs in Kenya:

  • Britam Money Market Fund
  • KCB M-Pesa (MMF option)
  • Zidii Money Market Fund

Benefits: Liquidity (easy withdrawals), low risk, steady returns (8–11% annually).

Stocks & Investments

Buying shares in a company makes you a part-owner of that business. Stocks can grow in value over time and may also pay dividends. They are higher-risk compared to SACCOs and MMFs but can bring higher returns.

Commonly Traded in Kenya:

  • Safaricom PLC
  • Equity Bank
  • KCB Group

Benefits: Potential for high returns, ownership in a company, dividend payments.

Pro-Tips from Your Financial Literacy Mentor

  • Start small — consistency matters more than size.
  • Diversify — combine SACCO, MMF, and stocks for balance.
  • Set financial goals and stick to them.
  • Always research before investing.
